The Security Challenge

Peace as the
Foundation

Mangu/Bokkos Federal Constituency has faced real and sustained security challenges — principally the farmer-herder conflict that has destabilised communities, disrupted agricultural livelihoods, and claimed lives. This is not a background issue. It is the defining challenge of our time in this constituency.

brings something no other candidate can offer: 35 years inside Nigeria's security architecture. He does not approach the security question as a politician making promises. He approaches it as a former Air Vice Marshal who has worked alongside the institutions, agencies, and command structures that determine how security is deployed and resourced across Nigeria.

His role as Chief of Logistics, HQ NAF gave him direct insight into how national security resources are allocated — and where the gaps are. He knows who to call. He knows what pressure to apply. And he knows that lasting peace is built through community trust, not just military presence.

"Security and lasting peace as a bedrock for all our individual and collective engagement — this is not negotiable. It comes first."

The Framework

A Five-Point
Security Plan

01
Federal Pressure & Advocacy
Use National Assembly platform and personal relationships within the security establishment to ensure adequate security deployment and resource allocation to Mangu/Bokkos.
02
Community Dialogue Facilitation
Establish structured, dignified dialogue frameworks between farming and herding communities — with traditional rulers, faith leaders, and women's groups as co-facilitators.
03
Early Warning Systems
Advocate for and help fund community early warning networks that can alert security agencies before tensions escalate into violence.
04
Legislative Action
Champion and support legislation at the House of Representatives that addresses the root causes of farmer-herder conflict — land use, grazing routes, and compensation frameworks.
05
Post-Conflict Recovery
Secure federal resources for community rebuilding — farm rehabilitation, displacement support, and psychosocial recovery programmes for affected families.

Inclusive Leadership

Working With
Everyone

AVM Masiyer has been explicit: this movement belongs to all of Mangu/Bokkos. He will work with every community group, every institution, and every stakeholder — because the challenges we face require collective action, not fractured politics.

👑
Traditional Rulers & Councils
The custodians of community authority and cultural heritage. AVM Masiyer commits to regular engagement with traditional rulers and district councils as primary partners in community development and peace.

Church & Faith Leaders
Plateau State's church networks are among the strongest forces for community cohesion and social support. AVM Masiyer will partner with faith leaders in community peace-building and development initiatives.

Women's Groups & Associations
Women hold communities together in times of crisis and drive economic activity in times of peace. Their organised networks are vital partners — in the campaign and in governance.

Youth Organisations

🌾
Farming Communities
The agricultural backbone of the constituency. AVM Masiyer will engage farmer cooperatives, associations, and individual smallholders directly — ensuring their priorities drive his National Assembly agenda.
🏘️
Community Leaders
Ward-level and LGA community leaders are the connective tissue of the constituency. AVM Masiyer's approach prioritises ward-level engagement as the foundation of credible representation.

Elderly, Disabled & Vulnerable
No one should be invisible to their representative. AVM Masiyer is committed to specific programmes for the elderly, people living with disability, and the most vulnerable members of our communities.

The Plan

Community
Engagement

AVM Masiyer's engagement approach is structured, sustained, and ward-level — not just rallies and town halls during election season. Here is how the campaign will reach and listen to every corner of Mangu/Bokkos.

Phase 1 — Now
Awareness & Introduction
Digital and WhatsApp campaign rollout. Introductory community visits. Engagement with traditional rulers and church leadership.
Phase 2 — Listening Tour
Ward-Level Engagement
Structured ward visits across all LGAs. Town halls with farming communities, women's groups, and youth associations. Listening first — speaking second.

Volunteer Network & Ward Coordinators

Full Campaign Mobilisation
Mass mobilisation. Endorsement events. Community rallies. Targeted voter registration drives and get-out-the-vote operations.

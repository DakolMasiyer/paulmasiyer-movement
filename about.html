Early Life & Origins

A Son of
Plateau State

Air Vice Marshal Paul D. Masiyer (Rtd.) was born and raised in Mangu/Bokkos Federal Constituency, Plateau State — the very land he now seeks to represent at the National Assembly. His roots in this constituency are not incidental. They are the reason he is running.

Growing up in Plateau State, AVM Masiyer developed a deep understanding of the aspirations and challenges of his people — the farming communities, the churches, the markets, the youth. That grounding never left him through three and a half decades of military service.

Military Career

AVM Masiyer joined the Nigerian Air Force and rose through the ranks with a combination of technical excellence, strategic thinking, and unwavering dedication to duty. Over the course of his career, he attained the rank of Air Vice Marshal — the peak of commissioned service — earning the respect of peers and superiors across the armed forces.

Among his most senior appointments was his role as Chief of Logistics, Headquarters Nigerian Air Force (HQ NAF), where he was responsible for the procurement, supply chain, and operational readiness systems that underpin the entire Air Force. Managing resources at this scale — ensuring thousands of personnel have what they need to defend the nation — demands the kind of precision, discipline, and integrity that defines AVM Masiyer's career.

He also served as Commandant of the Military Training Centre, shaping the next generation of Nigerian military officers. This role reflects not just his operational experience but his commitment to building capable, values-driven leaders — a commitment he now brings to public service.

"Having risen to the peak of military service, now brings his wealth of experience, discipline, and strategic leadership back home — to serve his people at a different level."

Why Politics. Why Now.

Retirement from the military did not mean retirement from service. For AVM Masiyer, the end of his uniform career was the beginning of a different calling — one that brings the discipline of national defence directly to the community level.

Mangu/Bokkos is a constituency with real and urgent needs: security challenges rooted in farmer-herder conflict, a farming economy that deserves federal support, young people with talent but limited opportunity, and communities that have long felt underrepresented in national conversations.

AVM Masiyer believes that a leader with his background — someone who has managed national logistics, commanded training institutions, and understood Nigeria's security architecture from the inside — is precisely what Mangu/Bokkos needs at the House of Representatives.

He is running under the platform of the All Progressives Congress (APC), aligned with the national agenda for security and development, and fully committed to working with all stakeholders — traditional rulers, faith leaders, women's groups, youth organisations, and farming communities — to reposition this constituency for peace, progress, and prosperity.
